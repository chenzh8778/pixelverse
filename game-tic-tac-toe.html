Review & Strategy

Tic-Tac-Toe, or Noughts and Crosses, is arguably one of the first strategy games anyone learns. Its 3x3 grid is a canvas for logic and foresight. While its strategic depth is limited, it serves as a perfect introduction to fundamental game theory concepts like optimal play, forcing moves, and recognizing unwinnable situations. It is the quintessential "solved game," yet it remains a beloved pastime.

Optimal Play

The strategy for Tic-Tac-Toe is well-established. To never lose, you must play optimally:

  1. First move: Take a corner. This gives you the most paths to victory.
  2. Responding: If your opponent takes the center, take the opposite corner. If they take any other square, take another corner.
  3. Winning/Blocking: From your third move onwards, your priority is simple: if you can make a line of three, do it. If not, check if your opponent can make a line of three on their next turn. If so, you must block them. If neither is possible, make a move that creates a new potential line for yourself.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is it possible to always win at Tic-Tac-Toe?

No. Tic-Tac-Toe is a 'solved game' where the outcome with perfect play is always a draw. Neither player can force a win against an opponent who also plays perfectly.

What is the best first move?

Playing in a corner square is generally considered the strongest opening move. It provides the most opportunities to create winning lines on your subsequent turns.
