Minesweeper

The ultimate game of logic and deduction. Don't step on a mine!

Mines: 10

Review & Strategy

Minesweeper is a legendary puzzle game that has been a staple of operating systems for decades. Unlike games of reflex, this is a game of pure logic. Each click is a calculated decision based on the information revealed. The suspense of clearing a large area, followed by the satisfaction of flagging the final mine, is a uniquely rewarding experience that no other game quite replicates.

Tips for Sweeping Mines

  • Start with the Corners: Clicking the corners first often reveals larger open areas, giving you more information to start with.
  • Learn the Patterns: You'll begin to recognize common number patterns. For example, a '1' on the edge of a cleared area with only one unknown square next to it *must* mean that unknown square is a mine. Flag it immediately.
  • Use Your Flags: Don't try to remember where all the mines are. Use the right-click to flag suspected mines. This clears your mental workspace and helps you solve more complex areas.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What do the numbers on the squares mean?

A number on a square indicates exactly how many mines are present in the eight squares immediately surrounding it. Use these numbers to deduce which adjacent squares are safe and which contain mines.

How do you flag a mine?

In this version, you can right-click on a square to place a flag, marking it as a suspected mine. Right-click again to remove the flag. Flagging helps you keep track of potential dangers.
